AI-Powered Materials Design: A Joint Embedding Approach
We tackled the challenge of designing complex molecules for advanced materials, where experimental data is scarce.
Our core innovation is **joint embedding**: We train our AI on both readily available data of simpler molecules *and* limited data of complex ones, focusing on shared **physicochemical features** to bridge the data gap.
This is achieved using a **Junction Tree Variational Autoencoder (JTVAE)**, which learns these shared patterns in the physicochemical properties.
We then explore design possibilities using **local ascent optimization**, refining existing molecular blueprints to unlock enhanced properties, guided by the learned embedding.

We successfully identified promising new molecules predicted to have superior performance using this combined approach.
Independent computational checks validated our AI's predictions, confirming the effectiveness of our approach.

AI-Powered Material Design : Shock Compression of Solids
Tackling Data Limitations: Pioneered machine learning models to predict the shock Hugoniot of solids, overcoming the challenges of limited experimental data.
Innovative Feature Development: Introduced and compared various feature engineering approaches, including a novel feature augmentation method, to enhance model accuracy with scarce data.
Model Selection and Analysis: Rigorously evaluated different machine learning models, identifying Gaussian process regression as the most effective for this application.
Data Variance Insights: Investigated how variations and nonlinearities in shock Hugoniot data affect model performance, providing guidelines for data quality.
Data Combination Strategy: Showcased the benefits of combining diverse datasets to improve model generalization and performance in data-scarce scenarios.
Design Applications: Demonstrated the practical application of these machine learning techniques in the design and discovery of materials for shock compression applications.

AI-Powered Material Design : Structure-Property Prediction in Organic Photovoltaics
**Multi-fidelity Framework:** Developed a novel deep learning framework that leverages both low- and high-fidelity simulations for accurate structure-property predictions in organic electronics.
**Data Efficiency:** Achieved significant reduction in the reliance on expensive high-fidelity data by effectively integrating readily available low-fidelity data.
**Model Architecture:** Employed a two-step approach involving an autoencoder for dimensionality reduction and multi-fidelity convolutional neural networks for property prediction, demonstrating superior performance over single-fidelity models.
**Application to OPVs:** Successfully applied the framework to predict the performance of organic photovoltaic (OPV) devices based on their microstructure.
**Generalizability:** Highlighted the potential of the approach for accelerated material quantification and discovery in various applications beyond organic electronics.

Elasticity and Size Effects on Li-ion Battery Electrodes
**Electrochemical Response:** The study explores the interplay of elasticity and particle size on the electrochemical response of a cylindrical graphite electrode in Li-ion batteries.
**Stress-Diffusion Coupling:** Simulations show a two way interaction between stress and diffusion, significantly affecting the filling of the electrode and resulting in higher current fluxes.
**Stress Effects:** The research reveals that stress-induced diffusion not only affects mechanical integrity but also alters the electrodeâ€™s electrochemical response to an applied potential.
**Particle Size Variations:** The investigation reveals a variation of stress effects with particle sizes in both discharge-charge and charge-discharge regimes, with maximum effects observed at different sizes for these two.
**Simulation Tools:** COMSOL and Mathematica were used for numerical simulations and analysis in this research.

RNN Encoder-Decoder for Time Series Prediction
**Real-time Prediction:** Developed an RNN Encoder-Decoder model for real-time time series prediction, specifically for predicting acceleration data.
**10-Step Ahead Prediction:** The model predicts values 10 time steps ahead, making it suitable for applications requiring early event detection like collision or explosion.
**Many-to-Many Model:** Utilizes a many-to-many RNN architecture to capture dependencies between time steps, resulting in improved prediction accuracy for high-rate sampled data.
**TensorFlow Implementation:** Implemented the model using TensorFlow, leveraging its capabilities for building and training complex neural networks.
**Offline Simulation of Real-time:** The code simulates real-time prediction by processing data sequentially, mimicking the behavior of an online system.
**Evaluation:** The model's performance is evaluated using Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE), and results are visualized through plots of predicted vs. actual values.
**Inspired by Research:** The implementation draws inspiration from a blog post on multivariate time series forecasting using seq2seq in TensorFlow.
**Customizable:** The code can be adapted for different datasets by modifying parameters such as filename, input data size, lookback size, and the number of time steps to process.
**Mean and Standard Deviation:** Calculates the mean and standard deviation of predictions at each time step to provide a measure of prediction confidence.
